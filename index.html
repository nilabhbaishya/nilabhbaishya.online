Travelling to Northeast India

When people think about traveling in India, destinations like Goa, Rajasthan, Kerala, or Himachal Pradesh often come to mind. But tucked away in the eastern corner of the country lies a region that remains one of India's best-kept secrets—Northeast India. Blessed with misty mountains, crystal-clear rivers, lush green valleys, vibrant cultures, and warm-hearted people, Northeast India offers an experience unlike anywhere else in the country. If you're looking for an offbeat adventure away from crowded tourist spots, this region deserves a place on your travel bucket list. .

Why Visit Northeast India?

The Northeast is made up of eight beautiful states—Assam, Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram, Tripura, and Sikkim. Each state has its own unique charm, traditions, food, and landscapes. Whether you enjoy trekking through mountains, exploring waterfalls, witnessing local festivals, or simply relaxing in nature, Northeast India has something for everyone. .

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Northeast India is between October and April.

October to November

This is one of the most beautiful seasons. The monsoon has ended, the skies are clear, and the landscapes are fresh and green. It's perfect for sightseeing and photography.

December to February

Winter brings cool and pleasant weather. Snow can be seen in higher-altitude regions like Tawang and parts of Sikkim. This season is great for mountain lovers.

March to April

Spring transforms the region into a colorful paradise. Flowers bloom across the hills, temperatures remain comfortable, and outdoor activities become even more enjoyable.

When to Avoid?

The monsoon season, from June to September, can bring heavy rainfall, landslides, and travel disruptions in some areas. While the scenery is stunning during this time, it may not be ideal for first-time visitors. .

Northeast India

Must-Visit Destinations

  • Shillong, Meghalaya

    Known as the "Scotland of the East," Shillong offers rolling hills, pleasant weather, waterfalls, and a lively local culture. Don't miss Elephant Falls and Umiam Lake.

  • Cherrapunji, Meghalaya

    Famous for being one of the wettest places on Earth, Cherrapunji is home to breathtaking waterfalls, caves, and living root bridges.

  • Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh

    Surrounded by snow-capped mountains, Tawang is peaceful and known for its monasteries and beautiful views.

  • Kaziranga National Park, Assam

    Home to the world's largest population of one-horned rhinoceroses. Perfect for wildlife safari experiences.

  • Gangtok, Sikkim

    A mix of modern city life and Himalayan beauty with amazing views, monasteries, and lakes nearby.

  • Ziro Valley, Arunachal Pradesh

    Peaceful valley known for nature, tribal culture, and the famous Ziro Music Festival.

Food You Must Try

• Momos & Thukpa
• Assamese Thali
• Smoked Pork (Nagaland)
• Jadoh (Meghalaya)
• Bamboo Shoot Dishes

Travel Tips

Carry warm clothes, cash, respect local culture, and travel slowly to enjoy nature.

Final Thoughts

Northeast India is not just a destination; it's an experience. From the emerald hills of Meghalaya to the majestic mountains of Arunachal Pradesh and the vibrant cultures spread across the region, every journey here leaves lasting memories. If you're seeking untouched beauty, authentic experiences, and a deeper connection with nature, Northeast India might just become your favorite corner of India. Pack your bags, keep your camera ready, and prepare to discover a side of India that many travelers have yet to explore. .
